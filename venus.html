Venus - The Earth's Twin

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" because of their similar size, mass, and composition. However, Venus has a toxic atmosphere and extreme surface temperatures.

Key Facts about Venus:

  • Type: Terrestrial Planet
  • Distance from the Sun: 108.2 million kilometers
  • Diameter: 12,104 kilometers
  • Surface Temperature: Up to 471°C (880°F)
  • Atmosphere: Mostly carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid
  • Rotation: Retrograde, meaning it spins in the opposite direction to most planets

Despite its similarities to Earth, Venus is a hostile environment with an atmosphere 92 times thicker than Earth's, resulting in a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the Solar System.
