Mars - The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance, caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

Key Facts about Mars:

  • Type: Terrestrial Planet
  • Distance from the Sun: 227.9 million kilometers
  • Diameter: 6,779 kilometers
  • Surface Temperature: -63°C (-81°F) average
  • Atmosphere: Thin, mostly carbon dioxide
  • Moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)

Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System (Olympus Mons) and the longest canyon (Valles Marineris). Scientists are particularly interested in Mars because it may have once had liquid water and possibly microbial life.
