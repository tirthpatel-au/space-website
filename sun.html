Sun - The Heart of Our Solar System

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, with internal convective motion that generates a magnetic field via a dynamo process.

Key Facts about the Sun:

  • Type: Yellow Dwarf Star (G-type main-sequence star)
  • Age: 4.6 billion years
  • Diameter: 1.39 million kilometers (109 times that of Earth)
  • Temperature: Surface temperature of 5,500°C (9,932°F)
  • Energy: Generates energy through nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium
  • Importance: Provides the energy needed for life on Earth

The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in the Solar System. It has an immense influence on our planet, controlling weather patterns, climate, and providing light and warmth for all living organisms on Earth.
